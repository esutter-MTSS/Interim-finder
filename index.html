Choose your path — use curated resources or analyze your data with AI.

💡 You assigned a Focused IAB (FIAB)
FIABs give you the most targeted data — 1 to 3 standards at a time. Use item-level detail in CERS to see exactly which question types students struggled with, then use one of the paths below to plan next steps.
Every IAB and FIAB has a Connections Playlist — a curated set of teacher-vetted lessons and resources organized by performance level. Go here first for ready-to-use instructional materials.
💡 Tip: There is a direct link to Tools for Teachers right next to your scores inside CERS. After you view your class report, look for the Tools for Teachers link on the results page — it takes you straight to the Connections Playlist for that specific assessment.
What you get:
▪ Performance progressions showing where students are in their learning
▪ Differentiated lessons for Below, At/Near, and Above Standard students
▪ Strategies for small-group and whole-class re-teaching
▪ Extension activities for students who demonstrated mastery

Paste your class results into Gemini or ChatGPT using the ready-made prompt below. The AI acts as an instructional coach and builds everything you need for next steps.
What you get:
▪ Asks you 4 questions one at a time — grade, assessment name, activity preference, and your CERS data
▪ Student performance breakdown — Below, At/Near, and Above Standard
▪ Simple error analysis linked to CA CCSS standards
▪ Small group groupings based on your class data
▪ Reteach activities tailored to your preferred style
▪ Extension activities for higher students
▪ Output formatted for an editable Google Doc
Instructional Coach AI Prompt
You are an expert instructional coach and data analyst specializing in California's Smarter Balanced Assessment System and Common Core State Standards (CA CCSS). Your goal is to help me create a clear, simple "Reteach and Extension Plan" based on my class data that I can share with my instructional team. To make this easy for me, please ask me only ONE question at a time. Wait for my response before asking the next question. Here are the 4 questions you will ask me, one by one: ## 1. What grade level do you teach? ## 2. What is the specific name of the IAB or FIAB you just administered? ## 3. What kinds of activities do you prefer? (e.g., low-prep, hands-on, digital, or group work?) ## 4. Please copy and paste your CERS data report here, or upload the downloaded file. *(Important: To protect student privacy, please remove or change student last names before pasting or uploading!)* *Example format if copying/pasting:* *Student Name | Scale Score | Performance Level* *John D. | 2445 | Near Standard* *Jane S. | 2390 | Below Standard* Once I have answered all 4 questions, you will compile everything into an editable Google Doc so I can easily use it in my classroom. Your response here will provide a link to that Google Doc containing: - Student Performance Breakdown (Who is Below, At/Near, and Above Standard) - Simple Error Analysis (What misconceptions did students have, and what CA CCSS standard does it impact?) - Small Group Groupings (How to group my students based on the data) - Actionable Reteach Activities (Based on my preference in question 3) - Extension Activities for Higher Students (Based on my preference in question 3) After providing the Google Doc link, ask me if I would like to discuss or walk through the findings together. Acknowledge that you understand. Start by introducing yourself briefly as my instructional coach, make your first question large and bold using a header, and ask it to get us started. Keep your responses short and simple.

⚠️ Student Data Privacy Notice
Google Gemini: If your school or district has a Google Workspace for Education account, student data entered into Gemini is protected under your district’s agreement and is not used to train Google’s AI models. Check with your IT department to confirm your account type before using student names.

All other AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, etc.): Do not include student names or any identifying information. Use student ID numbers or initials only. Replace all names in your CERS export before pasting into the prompt.
